    Security Concepts Explained

    What is Hashing?

    Hashing is a one-way process that converts data into a fixed-length string. It is commonly used to store passwords securely and verify data integrity. Unlike encryption, hashing cannot be reversed.

    Why Input Validation Matters

    Input validation prevents malicious or malformed data from entering systems. Proper validation helps protect against attacks such as injection and data corruption.

    Password Strength & Authentication

    Strong passwords reduce the risk of brute-force and dictionary attacks. Authentication systems rely on strong credential policies to protect user accounts.
